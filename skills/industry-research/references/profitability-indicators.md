# Profitability Indicators

Core question: who in the value chain captures profit, why, and is that profit distribution stable?

## Accounting and Financial Indicators

| Indicator | What it helps judge |
|---|---|
| Gross margin | Pricing power and direct cost pressure. |
| Net margin | Final profitability after expenses. |
| Expense ratios | Where resources are consumed. |
| ROE, ROA, ROIC | Capital efficiency and return quality. |
| Operating cash flow | Whether accounting profit converts into cash. |
| Accounts receivable | Customer bargaining power and collection risk. |
| Accounts payable | Supplier bargaining power and ability to occupy upstream funds. |
| Advances received / contract liabilities | Ability to collect cash before delivery. |
| Inventory turnover | Demand quality, channel pressure, and working-capital burden. |
| Capex | Capacity expansion, technology renewal, or competitive escalation. |
| Capacity utilization | Supply-demand balance and fixed-cost absorption. |

## Industry Structure Indicators

Use concentration, supply-demand balance, scarcity of key links, technical difficulty, know-how, standardization, customer concentration, supplier concentration, and channel concentration.

## Profit Pool Analysis

Map each value-chain link by revenue size, gross margin, capital intensity, working-capital power, supply-demand status, and strategic control point.

## Bargaining Power

Assess whether counterparties have choices. Customer alternatives weaken pricing power. Supplier scarcity raises input pressure. Channel control can take profit share. Ability to occupy upstream/downstream funds signals working-capital power.

## Top and Bottom Signals

Bottom signals: leaders reduce capacity, weak players exit, sell-side attention cools, inventory and prices stabilize after clearing.

Top signals: leaders expand capacity, outside players rush in, capex surges, expectations jump, inventory pressure builds while demand weakens.

## Output Rules

Explain which value-chain link captures profit, separate structural and cyclical profitability, link margin/cash-flow claims to data, and mark unavailable data as hypothesis.
