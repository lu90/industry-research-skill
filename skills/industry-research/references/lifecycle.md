# Lifecycle Judgment

Judge lifecycle before the seven core modules. The lifecycle stage determines analysis weight.

| Stage | Typical signs | Analysis focus |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | Low penetration, uncertain demand, unproven business model, technology or policy uncertainty | Feasibility, external factors, technical maturity. |
| Growth | Rapid penetration, supply shortage or fast capacity expansion, user diffusion, high revenue growth | Scalability, prosperity, competition, market size. |
| Maturity | Slower growth, stable demand center, repeat purchase, share competition, cost optimization | Profitability, defensibility, valuation, cycle position. |
| Decline | Substitution, shrinking demand, price sensitivity, cash-flow harvesting | Substitution risk, cash flow, low valuation trap. |

## Evidence Checklist

Use penetration rate, market growth rate, user diffusion stage, supply-demand balance, competitive structure change, valuation logic, prosperity indicators, and policy/technology shocks.

## Weighting Rules

- Introduction: emphasize feasibility, PEST, technology maturity, and whether demand is real.
- Growth: emphasize market size, prosperity indicators, scale economies, and competitive position.
- Maturity: emphasize profitability, defensibility, profit pools, valuation, and cycle.
- Decline: emphasize substitution, cash flow, asset value, and valuation traps.

## Output Format

State lifecycle conclusion, key evidence, counter-evidence, and confidence level. If evidence is mixed, describe a transition stage instead of forcing one label.
