# Seven Core Analysis Modules

Use these after the industry map and lifecycle judgment.

## 1. Feasibility

Can the business model work? Analyze real demand, use frequency, demand inelasticity, unit economics, and prerequisite technology/infrastructure/policy/user behavior.

## 2. Scalability

How large can the market become? Analyze TAM/SAM/SOM, demand-driven calculation, supply-driven calculation, supply-demand matching, and segmentation granularity.

## 3. Defensibility

Can incumbents defend their position? Analyze production factors, production relations, switching costs, network effects, substitutes, and technology disruption.

## 4. Profitability

Who captures profit in the value chain? Read `profitability-indicators.md`. Analyze profit pools, bargaining power, gross margin, cost structure, working-capital power, concentration, and supply-demand structure.

## 5. Valuation

What valuation logic fits this lifecycle stage? Read `valuation-logic.md`. Analyze stage-specific valuation logic, fundamentals vs liquidity/risk appetite, segment valuation, and valuation traps.

## 6. External Factors

Use PEST: political/policy, economic, social-cultural, technological. Focus on whether each factor is a driver, bottleneck, trigger, or valuation shock.

## 7. Prosperity

Is the industry improving or weakening now? Read `prosperity-indicators.md`. Analyze volume, price, inventory, cost, profit, cash flow, and forward-looking indicators.

## Cross-Module Rules

- Do not analyze all modules with equal weight. Weight by lifecycle and user question.
- Use the industry map as input to defensibility, profitability, and prosperity.
- Mark weak modules as evidence gaps rather than filling them with generic text.
