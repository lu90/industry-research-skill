# Capital Market Question

Use this reference only when the user asks about stock price movement, valuation, market expectation, investability, market-cap repair, or whether a listed company may rise or fall. Do not load this file for ordinary industry overviews or ordinary company/product positioning questions.

## Trigger Examples

Read this file when the user asks:

- Why did the stock fall or rise recently?
- Can the stock rise later?
- Why did valuation compress or expand?
- Why does the market not buy the story?
- Is the company expensive or cheap?
- Can market cap repair?
- Why did good earnings lead to a stock decline?

## Required Framing

- State that the output is research analysis, not investment advice.
- Do not give exact buy/sell points, guaranteed returns, or short-term trading instructions.
- Separate company fundamentals, industry beta, valuation logic, market expectation, and liquidity/risk appetite.
- Use `assets/company-product-template.md` as the base skeleton for standard and deep reports.
- Keep `11. 资本市场表现与估值预期变化` and `11.1-11.4` for capital-market questions.
- Do not replace the standard report with a three-part quick-comment structure such as "why it fell", "what to watch", and "my judgment".

## Incremental Modules

Add these modules on top of company/product analysis:

1. Stock performance decomposition: price move, time window, benchmark/index comparison, and known catalysts when available.
2. Fundamental change: revenue, margin, cash flow, delivery/order/operating metrics, guidance, and business mix.
3. Industry and macro beta: sector cycle, policy, rates, risk appetite, commodity/cost pressure, demand cycle.
4. Valuation logic: current valuation anchor, multiple compression/expansion, segment valuation, profit expectations, growth assumptions.
5. Expectation gap: what the market previously priced in, what changed, and what must be proven next.
6. Upside triggers: measurable events that could support repair.
7. Downside risks: measurable events that could invalidate the recovery thesis.
8. Scenario analysis: bullish/base/bearish conditions and indicators to track.

## Evidence Rules

- Run a first-pass primary-source retrieval before using media summaries. Use `references/primary-source-first.md` for the evidence hierarchy and disclosure pattern.
- First try to identify the listed company ticker, listing venue, and exact time window for the price move.
- Use company filings, IR, announcements, and exchange filings for financial and operating facts.
- Use exchange or credible market data for price and valuation facts when available.
- Use broker/consensus/media only as opinion or supplementary signal unless independently verified.
- If market data is unavailable, mark it as an evidence gap and discuss the required verification source.
- If only secondary sources are available in the active environment, explicitly disclose in `2.2 来源矩阵和证据质量` which primary sources were attempted or should be checked next.
