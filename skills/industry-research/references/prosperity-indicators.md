# Prosperity Indicators

Use this file to choose indicators that reveal whether an industry is improving or weakening.

## Universal Dimensions

| Dimension | Example indicators |
|---|---|
| Volume | Sales, orders, shipments, production, utilization, traffic, active users. |
| Price | Terminal price, wholesale price, raw material price, component price, service price. |
| Inventory | Channel inventory, dealer inventory, company inventory, social inventory. |
| Cost | Raw materials, labor, energy, logistics, customer acquisition cost. |
| Profit | Gross margin, net margin, unit economics. |
| Cash flow | Collections, dealer payments, account period, advances received. |
| Forward-looking | Capex, order backlog, production scheduling, hiring, financing, policy change. |

## Industry-Type Indicator Library

- Consumer: retail sales, same-store sales, foot traffic, basket size, repeat purchase, channel inventory, discount rate, brand search/social interest.
- Manufacturing: PMI, orders, backlog, utilization, production schedule, equipment investment, raw/component cost, delivery cycle.
- Cyclical commodities: product price, spread, inventory, utilization, new capacity/shutdowns, imports/exports, futures curve.
- Internet platforms: DAU/MAU, time spent, GMV, take rate, ad price, CAC, retention, concentration.
- SaaS/software: ARR/MRR, net revenue retention, churn, bookings, pipeline, gross margin, sales efficiency, payback period.
- Healthcare: patient volume, prescription volume, hospital procurement, reimbursement policy, clinical progress, approval pipeline, channel inventory.
- Real estate/infrastructure: land transactions, new starts, sales area, completion, inventory months, mortgage rates, infrastructure investment, funding.
- Financial: interest rates, credit growth, AUM, trading volume, net interest margin, NPL ratio, risk appetite, regulation.
- New energy/automotive: monthly sales, penetration, battery/raw material prices, charging/swapping infrastructure, dealer inventory, discounts, export volume, production schedule.

## Use Rules
